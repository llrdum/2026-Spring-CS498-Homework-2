=== Discussion ===
Instance A (us-central1) shows lower latency (~121ms for /register, ~106ms 
for /list) compared to Instance B (europe-west1), which averaged ~458ms and ~396ms 
respectively. Instance B is roughly 3.7x slower for /register and 3.7x slower for 
/list, reflecting the greater physical distance between our client and the european 
region, where transatlantic network round-trip time is the dominant factor.

The eventual consistency experiment produced a surprising result: 0 out of 200 
iterations failed to find the username immediately on Instance B after registering on 
Instance A. This suggests that in practice, Firestore's replication between us-central1 
and europe-west1 is fast enough that by the time our /list request completes its full 
round trip (~396ms), the data has already propagated. This shows that while eventual 
consistency does not guarantee immediate visibility in theory, Firestore's replication 
lag in practice can be shorter than the network latency itself, making inconsistencies 
difficult to observe under normal conditions.
